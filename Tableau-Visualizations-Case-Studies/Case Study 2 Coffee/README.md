#  Case Study 2 – Coffee Chain Dataset

###  Objective
To analyze sales, profit, and marketing performance across different coffee products and regions using Tableau visualizations.

---

###  Dataset
`Coffee_Chain.csv`

---

###  Tasks Performed
1. Heat Map – Product Type, State, and Profit  
2. Box Plot – Espresso Sales Distribution  
3. Bar Chart – Profit by Product and Type  
4. Scatter Plot – Sales vs Profit by State  
5. Profit Comparison – Highest & Lowest Profit States  
6. Sales in Nevada for Decaf Espresso  
7. Tea’s Contribution to Overall Profit (%)  
8. Average Marketing (Area Codes 660 & 818)  
9. Product Profit (Highest & Lowest in California)  
10. Side-by-side Circles – Minimum Marketing for Colombian Coffee  
11. East Market Sales % for Decaf  
12. 2012 East Market Sales % for Decaf  
13. Average Profit for Products Starting with ‘C’  
14. Central Market – Top 5 Product Sales Contribution (%)  
15. 2013 West Market – Highest Profit State  
16. Expenses-to-Sales Ratio for Lowest Profit State  
17. Distinct Area Codes for Lowest Budget Margin (Small Markets)  
18. 2013 Major Market – Profit % for Caffe Mocha  
19. Month & Year where Decaf Sales Crossed $1100 (Colorado & Florida)  
20. Tree Map – Market with Maximum Budget Sales  

---

###  Key Insights
- **Lowest Profit (Espresso):** New Hampshire (-375)  
- **Highest Sales Distribution (Espresso):** Regular Espresso (~815)  
- **Top Profitable Products:** Colombian Coffee & Caffe Mocha  
- **Highest Profit State (2013 – West):** California (~18.82K)  
- **Lowest Profit State:** New Mexico (~ -799)  
- **Tea’s Profit Contribution:** ~20.41%  
- **Average Marketing (Area Codes 660 & 818):** ~$32.89  
- **Average Profit (Products starting with “C”):** 71.85%  
- **Top 5 Products (Central Region):** 55.52% of total sales  
- **Major Market (Caffe Mocha 2013):** 3.78% Profit Share  
- **Tree Map Insight:** Central Market had maximum budget sales (~149,200)
